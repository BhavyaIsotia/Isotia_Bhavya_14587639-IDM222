Poems That Hold What I Cannot Say Out Loud

Poetry is where memory sits quietly and speaks. These pieces hold the things I cannot always name: the people I miss, versions of myself I’ve outgrown, humor I cling to, and myths I rewrite.

Format · Free Verse & Narrative Poems Themes · Memory, Identity, Humor, Myth Author · Bhavya Isotia

Overview

Poetry, for me, is a place where memory sits quietly and speaks. It is where I store the things I cannot name, the people I miss, and the worlds I imagine when reality feels too sharp. This collection gathers poems written across different moments, some tender, some humorous, some aching, some curious. They live here the way artifacts do: kept, revisited, softened by touch.

Mother, Memory & Holding On

Wrapped in Her Scent

A poem about distance, scent memory, and the quiet ways mothers keep us tethered to home.

It happened in a rush
 my bag split open
 right at the curb,
 just as I was leaving home again.
 Without thinking,
 my mother took the scarf
 from around her neck
 and tied it tight
 around the suitcase
 a strong knot of silk and instinct.
 She said nothing,
 handed the bag back,
 brushed hair from my face
 like she always does.
 I took it.
 I left.
 Hours later,
 across oceans and cities,
 I noticed the scent
 her perfume, still alive
 in the folds of fabric.
 Lavender, when I held it close,
 powdery and sweet when I was lonely,
 citrusy and sharp when
 I wanted to cry for no clear reason.
 The scent shifted with me,
 like it knew what I needed
 Just like how she did.
 The scarf is sage green,
 tiny white specks like dust
 on old family photos.
 Its edges are frayed,
 threads curling together, hugging.
 She wore it light,
 tied with grace,
 like it was part of her breath.
 I wear it clenched
 a little too tight,
 like I’m trying to hold her in place.
 Sometimes around my neck,
 sometimes my wrist,
 when my fingers feel lost.
Sometimes I wear it
to feel her arms around me
still within reach.
Other times,
I panic
what if the smell fades?
What if I wear it too much
and lose her completely?
I imagine folding it carefully,
tucking it in a box
lined with tissue paper,
sealing the scent inside
like a secret I’m not ready to lose.
But what good is a hug
locked away?
What good is keeping safe
if it means never touching again?
So I keep it near.
Not every day
but when the air feels too big
and I need something small and certain
I find the scarf,
breathe in her name,
even if it’s only
a whisper now.

Humor, Loss & Small Creatures We Name

In Memory of Gus Gus

In Memory of Gus Gus
by Bhavya Isotia
It started with a scream.
 Not the horror movie kind…
 more like “what was that?”
 followed by group texts titled MOUSE ALERT
 in all caps, because lowercase
 didn’t feel urgent enough.
He was small.
 But strategic.
 Avoided every trap
 with the precision of a tiny criminal
 trained in urban survival.
We tried peanut butter.
 Then cheese.
 Then… a banana?
 Nothing worked.
 So we gave in.
Named him Gus Gus.
 Like Cinderella’s sidekick,
 except he didn’t sew dresses
 just tore through our garbage
 at 2:14 a.m. every other Tuesday.
Soon, it became routine.
 A clank in the kitchen,
 a scuttle near the stove.
 “I think it’s Gus Gus,”
 my roommate would mumble,
 already half-asleep,
 and we’d just roll over,
 accepting the chaos
 like he paid rent.

He was
 kind of family.
 The fourth roommate
 we never officially approved
 but slowly got used to.
Then one day
 caught.
 Whisked away in a box
 with air holes
 and zero fanfare.
 “Got him,” the rodent control man said.
The silence was too clean.
 We were relieved,
 bleached the counter twice,
 celebrated with overpriced takeout.
But some nights,
 when it’s too quiet,
 I hear a rustle in the trash
 that isn’t real
 and miss him a little.
 Just a little.

Identity, Culture & Being Seen

Limited Edition: For Display Only

Limited Edition: For Display Only
by Bhavya Isotia
Congratulations!
  you’ve unlocked the Limited Edition Model™.
She comes fully assembled
 with multilingual settings (set to mute),
 ancestral spice memory,
 and default guilt mode pre-installed.
Her name is Bhavya,
 but don’t worry
 you’ll rename her in the first five minutes.
 Something easier.
 Something that fits on your tongue
 like plain toast on a paper plate.
She will answer anyway.
Her packaging is vintage:
 bruised brass, sandalwood threads,
 smells like visa stamps and cardamom breath
 She folds herself into carry-on dimensions.
 Lightweight.
 TSA approved.
 Does not trigger alarms.
She has two sets of outfits:
 One for home,
 wrapped in turmeric and noise,
 and one for out,
 ironed flat
 for professional use.
Please enjoy responsibly.
Caution:
 Exposure to microaggressions
 may cause her to speak in perfect English
 or switch her accent mid-sentence.
In rare cases,
 she may malfunction-
 pronounce her name correctly,
 correct your pronunciation,
 speak her full name into a microphone.
We don’t recommend that.
She was built for aesthetics,
 not resistance.
Please do not ask her to dance unless
 you’ve paid for lessons
 or colonialism.
Do not ask for recipes
 and then gag at the smell.
Do not tell her
 you once had a friend from India
 and think that counts.
This model is Limited Edition.
 And no, there won’t be another.
Because even when you shelf her
 next to your “World Market” candles
 and call it cultural appreciation,
 she rolls her eyes… in perfect rhythm.
She remembers
 every ingredient you couldn’t pronounce,
 every nickname you invented like it was a game,
 every “exotic” label stuck on her like clearance tags.
She remembers.
 And this time-
 she comes with no return policy.

Myth, Reflection & Loneliness

This Is Not My Voice

"This Is Not My Voice"
by Echo (sort of)
"Shall I compare thee…"
 no. wait. that’s not…
 “Love looks not with the eyes…”
 ugh.
I borrowed words
but not a voice.
You never heard me.
Just your name bouncing off cave walls.
Your name,
Your name,
Your
I’ve tried to leave this message before.
 But it always loops.
 Rewinds.
 echos on a half-formed syllable.
You stared at the lake,
 I stared at you.
 And both of us
 fell in love
 with a reflection.
Do you remember when I almost said something real?
 When you turned toward the sound,
 not the source?
 That was me.
 Almost me.
You want to be understood.
 I want to be misquoted.
 Heard wrong,
 but heard
 loud.
They say you drowned.
 I say you
 folded inward like a napkin
 with your face pressed into
 your own idea of beauty.
I don't blame you.
I watched myself
fade into
your silence.
I wanted to say:
 I see you.
 But the words shattered
 mid-throat.
And now?
 I hope.
 I mimic birds,
 repeat songs and lines,
 mouth the world
 until the echo
 finally sounds like me.
This is a message.
 A message.
 A mess.

Time, Fear & Waiting Rooms

Time Slot Pending

“Time Slot Pending”
At a summer fair,
 I asked a tarot reader how long I’d live.
 She handed me an email address:
 time.appointments@fate.net
 said, “He’ll know more than I do.”
I sent a polite message.
 Got a reply:
 “Your time slot is tentative.
 Subject to
 change without warning.”
Curiosity got the best of me.
 The office was between a laundromat
 and a clock repair shop
 or maybe it just looked that way.
I signed in.
 Sat in the waiting room
 between a banana and a houseplant.
 “We expire differently,”
 the banana whispered.
 “But we all go soft eventually.”
The houseplant said nothing.
 Just dropped a yellowing leaf.
A receptionist with hourglass eyes
 offered me tea.
 I declined.
 (You never trust beverages in liminal spaces.)
Then, Time appeared.
 Corduroy blazer, coffee breath,
 a tired smile like he’s met everyone before.
 “Ready?” he asked,
 as if that’s a real question.
He didn’t tell me the date.
 Just smiled and said,
 “Don’t worry. You’ll know when it’s time.”
 Then turned the hourglass over.
The grains began to fall,
 but slower than I’d expected.

Work, Chaos & Office Humor

Best Secretary and Her Pink Slips

Best Secretary and Her Pink Slips
(its voice of a frazzled assistant)
While you were out:
 – Mr. Jones called. Something about a cat. Or a contract. Or both? He’s confusing. You should call. Or better don’t.
 – Your dentist moved the root canal to Tuesday. Congrats…you’ll have to cancel your date. Again.
 – Karen from HR wants her stapler back. Urgently. She used the word “hostile.”
 – Your mom says: “Don’t forget the lasagna this time.” Her tone was... sharp.
 – Facilities says your office plant is officially dead. You may need to hold a service.
 – Someone named “The Lizard King” dropped off a box. I didn’t sign. I’m scared of lizards.
Also:
 – I spilled some tea on message #4.
 – Someone left half a donut on your chair. I tried to ID the flavor. Couldn’t. Had to throw it away. You're welcome.
 – Your inbox is making a soft weeping noise. Unclear if it’s electronic or emotional.
 – You might have a rescheduled date Friday... or a disciplinary hearing. You know why. Could go either way.
I tried to keep it all straight —
 but the phones were ringing,
 the copier jammed itself out of protest,
 and someone was crying in the breakroom. Again.

Spirituality, Quantum Theory & Light

Particles of Truth

Particles of Truth
by Bhavya Isotia
असतो मा सद्गमय। तमसो मा ज्योतिर्गमय। मृत्योर्मा अमृतं गमय॥
(Lead me from untruth to truth, from darkness to light, from death to immortality.) ~ Brihadaranyaka Upanishad
In quantum theory, observation collapses probabilities;
the particle decides its state only when it is seen. ~ Quantum Mechanics: The Theoretical Minimum
I light the diya,
its flame flickering in the draft of my doubt,
tiny as a photon,
yet enough to cast shadows on the walls of my mind.
The mantra whispers:
Lead me from what is false to what endures.
Science murmurs:
Truth is a spectrum- entangled, unseen until observed.
Light takes eight minutes to reach us,
yet its warmth is already present…
like the truth that has always been,
waiting for my sight to catch up.
Stars forge iron in their hearts,
then explode, spilling light into dark.
Iron drifts into oceans, lungs, ink;
darkness folding into illumination.
I breathe, caught between these worlds.
The sage says:
Lead me from death to immortality.
Physics says:
Energy never dies, only changes form.
Time devours all,
but order remains:
laws of physics,
or karma encoded in the shape of our universe.
I close my eyes,
and let the worlds converge:
particle and photon,
mantra and flame.
Two forces: ancient and exact-
guiding me,
carrying truth, light, and life
through me,
beyond me.

Anxiety, Google Searches & Growing Up Alone

Google, I Need Answers

Google, I Need Answers
 by Bhavya Isotia
Dear Search,
I thought something was moving in my eye,
maybe a worm?
Search: “Do worms lay eggs in human eyes?”
Now my eyes are red from scrubbing too hard.
Or maybe I just fainted from panic?
Either way, how does my neighbor always know
the exact moment I have a boy over?
Are there hidden cameras?
Search: “How to check your house for wiretraps?”
I swear I hear her footsteps before she’s even outside.
And then my finger tingled.
Search: “Why does my pinky feel numb?”
Results: “Could be nerve damage, diabetes, or… cancer.”
I stared at my hand like it betrayed me.
Do I call a doctor or just agree with Mom and never search again?
Or the cereal. Every morning it’s gone, though I never eat it.
Search: “How to know if there’s a ghost in your apartment?”
I live alone, okay? Don’t judge me.
And the crush.
Search: “How to tell if someone likes you without asking.”
Why do my awkward decisions, tiny panics, and one-sided flutters
always feel like they deserve a Google search?
But hey, at least the Internet knows me
better than anyone else, honestly.
P.S. Google, search: “Condition when you walk or eat at night in sleep.”
P.P.S. I realized I don’t have a house ghost.
It’s always been me… and my Cinnamon Toast Crunch midnight ritual.
